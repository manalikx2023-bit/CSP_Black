How the AI Optimisation Model Works
The model uses regression analysis trained on 30,731 slab records and 4,344 hours of furnace data to predict FCE dropout temperature for any given zone temperature combination. The core objective is energy minimisation while guaranteeing FCE stays above the recipe target. It recommends lower zone setpoints that save energy without compromising slab quality.
Zone Setpoint Derivation - Important Clarification
Source Data Fact: The process parameter file contains zone temperatures (actual measured values), not separate setpoint columns. In steady-state furnace operation, the actual zone temperature closely reflects the operator's setpoint on the PLC/SCADA system.

Current SP (in this model): = actual measured zone temperature from data. This is what the furnace is currently achieving - effectively the current operating setpoint.

Model Recommended Temperature: = current zone temperature MINUS the reduction recommended by the model (always lower). This is the new setpoint the operator should enter in the PLC to achieve energy savings.

Example: Z2 currently at 1267 degC (actual). Model recommends 1212 degC. Operator sets PLC to 1212 degC. Furnace zone cools to ~1212 degC. Zone fuel drops proportionally. Energy saving achieved.
