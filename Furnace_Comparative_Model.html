Model M1 — Maximum Setpoint Strategy
Zone_SP = f(max(recipe_target_i) × zone_scale)
Ensuring: predict_FCE(slab_i) ≥ target_i − 3°C for ALL slabs
Zone SPs are derived from the highest recipe temperature in the current slab queue. All zones scale proportionally (Z1/Z2 preheat ×0.956, Z3/Z4 heating ×1.0, Z5–Z8 soaking ×0.968). Safest quality guarantee — no under-heating possible by design.
Conservative Zero underheating Higher fuel Simple logic
Model M2 — Weighted Average Setpoint Strategy
T_wt = Σ(mass_i × res_i × recipe_i) / Σ(mass_i × res_i)
Zone_SP = f(T_wt × zone_scale) — physics-balanced
Zone SPs are derived from a mass × residence-time weighted average of all slab recipe temperatures. Higher mass and longer residence time slabs carry more weight. Optimises fuel while balancing the heat distribution across all grades simultaneously.

When to Use Each Model
🔵 Use Model M1 (Max SP) When
  • High-value, high-spec grades (ANLNP41, ANCRMB6 premium) present
  • Wide recipe span in queue (>15°C gap between grades)
  • Cold-entry slabs (<100°C entry temp) form >20% of queue
  • Commissioning or furnace startup conditions
  • Quality audit period — zero underheating tolerance
  • Operator unfamiliar with grade mix behaviour
🟢 Use Model M2 (Weighted Avg) When
  • Stable, well-known grade mix with similar recipe targets
  • Recipe spread <10°C across all slabs in queue
  • Steady-state production with consistent entry temps
  • Energy cost reduction is primary objective
  • Model validation complete on this grade combination
  • Shift schedule is predictable 1–2 hours ahead
🟡 Use Hybrid (Adaptive Switch) When
  • Mixed queue: some slabs need max, others are routine
  • Apply M1 during first 30% of shift (cold furnace transition)
  • Switch to M2 after thermal equilibrium confirmed
  • Use M2 weighted avg + cold-slab Z7/Z8 compensation
  • Monitor FCE dropout in real time — trigger M1 if 2+ fails
  • Default for most production scenarios
